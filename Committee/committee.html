Hi, I'm Tim and I am a 2nd year PhD student in the mathematical biology group. My project, which is funded by the KESS2 scholarship and cofunded by the London Women's Clinic, looks at using time-lapse images of embryos thawing after vitrification to select the healthiest embryos which have the greatest chance of leading to pregnancy. I also investigate the way embryos warm up during cryopresevation, in order to validate handling protocols designed to prevent thermal damage. As one of the webmasters, feel free to contact me if there are any issues with the website!

Hi, I’m Emily and I’m in my fourth and final year of my PhD. I am based in the Operational Research group, focussing on applications in healthcare. My PhD is funded by a KESS2 scholarship and also the Welsh Blood Service, who are my industrial partner. The aim of my research is to create a mathematical model to optimise blood donation clinic and workforce scheduling to match supply and demand, and also to reduce wastage in the system.
